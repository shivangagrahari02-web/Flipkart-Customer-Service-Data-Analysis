# Flipkart Customer Service Data Analysis using Excel

# Overview

This project focuses on analyzing Flipkart’s customer service operations to understand how service quality impacts customer satisfaction and retention.

The analysis was performed using Excel by applying data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), pivot tables, correlation analysis, and interactive dashboarding on customer call data.

The project aimed to uncover operational inefficiencies, identify key customer pain points, and provide actionable recommendations to improve customer experience and retention.

---

# Problem Statement

Flipkart observed a decline in customer retention rates and wanted to investigate whether customer service performance was contributing to the issue.

The objective of this project was to analyze customer call data and evaluate:
- Customer satisfaction trends
- Response time efficiency
- Sentiment patterns
- Call center performance
- Channel effectiveness
- Issue distribution

The goal was to identify operational gaps affecting customer experience and recommend strategies to improve retention and service quality.

---

# Key Insights

- Customer sentiment showed a very high positive correlation with CSAT score (r ≈ 0.89).
- Billing-related issues contributed approximately 71.37% of total customer tickets.
- Response time and call duration showed minimal impact on customer satisfaction.
- Chatbot interactions showed relatively lower customer satisfaction compared to other channels.
- Fridays experienced the highest operational load and comparatively lower customer satisfaction.
- Customer experience was influenced more by issue resolution quality and sentiment rather than operational speed.

Key insights were derived through pivot table analysis, correlation studies, and dashboard visualization.

# Results & Conclusion

This project demonstrates how Excel can be used effectively for customer service analytics and business problem-solving.

The analysis revealed that:
- Customer sentiment and issue resolution quality are the strongest drivers of customer satisfaction.
- Operational speed metrics like response time and call duration have relatively lower impact on CSAT.
- Billing issues are the major contributor to customer dissatisfaction.
- Certain support channels and peak operational days negatively affect customer experience.

The project highlights the importance of customer experience quality over operational efficiency alone in improving customer retention.
